01 // The Problem

Platforms Are Multiplying.
Coordination Intelligence Isn't.

Today's battlefield runs hundreds of autonomous drones, robots, and sensors alongside human operators. Systems like ATAK and Delta give humans a shared map — but machines have no shared understanding. When the server link drops under electronic warfare, the picture disappears entirely.

01

SERVER DEPENDENCY

Current C4ISR (ATAK, Delta, Lattice) requires server infrastructure and continuous connectivity. Under electronic warfare — the picture vanishes. No degradation. Just gone.

02

HUMAN BOTTLENECK

When one drone sees a threat, a human must interpret it and relay it. When 50 drones each see something different, the human is the bottleneck. No machine-to-machine coordination intelligence exists.

03

SILOED PLATFORMS

Each vendor's drones coordinate only with their own drones. No cross-vendor, cross-domain coordination. Soldiers and machines exist on separate networks with no shared world model.

04

NO EDGE INTELLIGENCE

Coordination intelligence lives on servers, not on platforms. When comms degrade, platforms become isolated actors with no predictive capability and no shared understanding.

03 // Data Fidelity

Same World. Same Data.
For AI, MilSim = Real Ops.

Computer vision doesn't see lethality — it sees human poses, equipment textures, terrain features, vehicle dynamics. In every dimension that CV and multi-agent AI actually learn from, milsim data is functionally equivalent to real military operational data.

IDENTICAL — WHAT AI SYSTEMS LEARN FROM
  • Human kinematics & gear — tactical movement, genuine military equipment at pixel level
  • Vehicle & drone dynamics — real convoys, FPV approaches, recon patterns, evasion
  • Real terrain, weather, lighting, occlusion — zero rendering artifacts
  • Multi-agent coordination — 1,000+ actors, command hierarchies, fire-and-maneuver
  • Shared situational awareness — communication patterns, information flow across units
  • Swarm & human-drone teaming — 100+ drones, real operator handoffs, mixed autonomy
DIFFERENT — WHAT CV/AI DOESN'T LEARN FROM
  • Lethality of projectiles — invisible to CV, same pixels regardless
  • Acute stress physiology — does not affect visual features, kinematics, or spatial patterns
  • Internal weapon mechanisms — airsoft replicas cosmetically identical at CV resolution
  • Structural damage from ordnance — irrelevant to detection, tracking, coordination AI

05 // Real-World Data Capture

Closing Sim-to-Real Gap with Live Milsim Ops

Each participant carries a sensor stack — 360° cameras, GPS/IMU modules, and tactical radios — all locked to a shared satellite clock. This gives us frame-level temporal alignment across every data stream: video, telemetry, comms, and drone feeds. The result is a single unified spatiotemporal dataset where any moment can be reconstructed from every perspective simultaneously.

60-80 vehicles (SUVs, trucks) per event with onboard cameras, GPS, speed, and movement logs.

DRONE FEEDS + LOGS
